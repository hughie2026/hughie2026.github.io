About Me

I am an Associate Professor at the Macao University of Tourism and a Fellow of the Royal Society for Public Health (FRSPH). My work sits at the intersection of lifestyle medicine, data science, and travel medicine, applying decision science, causal inference, and AI-oriented analytics to public health problems with a focus on health systems, behavioral determinants, and the epidemiology of mobility.

Since dedicating myself to higher medical education in 2019, I have been deeply engaged in frontline teaching. To date, I have delivered over 1,000 hours of instruction to both undergraduate and postgraduate students. Throughout this journey, I have been committed to integrating foundational theoretical knowledge with cutting-edge clinical and research advancements. My goal is to help medical students at different stages build rigorous academic and clinical reasoning, laying a solid professional foundation for their medical careers.
