About

I am an Associate Professor at the University of Tourism, Macao. My academic work focuses on global health, social determinants, health systems and governance, AI-oriented decision science, data analytics, causal inference, and travel-related health research. This website presents my academic background, research interests, teaching, software outputs, and scholarly activities.

Research Interests

  • Global Health: Social Factors, Health Systems, and Governance
  • Decision Science, Data Analytics, and Causal Inference (AI-Oriented)
  • Medical Tourism, Travel Epidemiology, and Travel Medicine

Research Highlights

Global Health and Governance

Studying how social factors, institutions, and governance frameworks shape health outcomes and public health practice.

AI-Oriented Decision Science

Applying data analytics and causal inference to support evidence-based decision-making in health and tourism-related contexts.

Travel Medicine and Medical Tourism

Exploring health risks, behavioral patterns, and decision support approaches in travel epidemiology, travel medicine, and medical tourism.

Teaching

Courses taught include Applied Statistics, Health Management, Business Analytics, Nutrition, and Epidemiology.

Over the past three years, I undertook more than 500 teaching hours per academic year for undergraduate core courses. I also delivered postgraduate-level courses in Health Big Data and Medical Research Methodology.

I have supervised 35 undergraduate theses, co-supervised 4 master’s theses, and 1 doctoral dissertation.
